Question 2.

What is the distribution of backpack weights that students are carrying? Choose an appropriate graph to show the distribution.

#View(backpack)
backpack %>% 
  summarise(avg_backpack_weight = mean(backpack_weight),
            median_backpack_weight = median(backpack_weight),
            min_backpack_weight = min(backpack_weight),
            max_backpack_weight = max(backpack_weight))
backpack %>% 
  ggplot(aes(x = backpack_weight)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = 5, col = "white", boundary = 5) +
  theme_minimal()+
  labs(x = "\nBackpack Weight (lb)",
       y = "Count\n",
       title = "Backpack Weight by Five Pound Intervals")

  • Most commonly backpacks will weigh greater than 5 lb and less than 15 lb
  • The lightest is 2 lb.
  • The heaviest is 35 lb. It seems that this dataset is North American in origin as the likely units of weight are in pounds (lb). Backpack

Question 3.

Are there differences in backpack weight between sex?

backpack %>% 
  select(sex, backpack_weight) %>% 
  group_by(sex) %>% 
  summarise(count = n(),
            avg_backpack_weight = mean(backpack_weight)) %>% 
  ungroup()
backpack %>% 
  ggplot(aes(x = backpack_weight, fill = sex)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = 5, col = "white", boundary = 5) +
  facet_wrap(~sex)+
  theme_minimal()+
  labs(x = "\nBackpack Weight (lb)",
       y = "Count\n",
       title = "Backpack Weight by Five Pound Intervals and Sex",
       fill = "Sex")

* There are more females (55) than males (45). * Average backpack weight for females is 11.04 lb. * Average backpack weight for males is 12.42 lb. * More males carry backpacks in the 15-20 lb range than females. * More females carry backpacks in the 20-25 lb range than males. * Most common backpack weight for females is> 5 lb < 10 lb. * Most common backpack weight for males is > 10 lb < 15 lb.

Question 4.

What is the relationship between body weight and backpack weight?

backpack %>% 
  ggplot(aes(
    x = body_weight,
    y = backpack_weight)) +
  geom_point() + 
  labs(x = "Body Weight (lb)", y = "Backpack Weight (lb)")

  • The lightest backpacks are carried by body weights < 125 lb.
  • The heaviest backpacks are carried by body weights < 200 lb.
  • The heaviest body weights (> 200 lb) carry backpacks < 15 lb.

There is no clear overall pattern, as body weight increases backpack weights are mostly distributed fairly evenly above or below body weights. Individual strength would appear more relevant than body weight.
